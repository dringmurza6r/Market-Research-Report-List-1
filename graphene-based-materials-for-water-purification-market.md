<p><p>The graphene-based materials market for water purification primarily includes three types: graphene, graphene oxide, and reduced graphene oxide. Graphene, a single layer of carbon atoms, exhibits exceptional conductivity and surface area, making it effective for filtration. Graphene oxide, which contains oxygen functional groups, enhances water treatment capabilities by improving dispersion and interaction with contaminants. Reduced graphene oxide combines properties of both graphene and graphene oxide, offering a balance of efficiency and cost-effectiveness in removing pollutants from water sources.</p></p>
