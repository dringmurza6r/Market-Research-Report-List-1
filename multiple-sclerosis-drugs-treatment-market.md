<p><p>The Multiple Sclerosis (MS) drugs treatment market includes three primary types: Interferons, Immunomodulators, and Immunosuppressants. Interferons are proteins that modify immune responses to reduce inflammation and disease progression. Immunomodulators enhance the body's immune system to manage symptoms and improve function. Immunosuppressants, on the other hand, reduce or inhibit the immune response to prevent the immune system from attacking the nervous system. Each class serves distinct roles in managing MS and tailoring treatment to individual patient needs.</p></p>
