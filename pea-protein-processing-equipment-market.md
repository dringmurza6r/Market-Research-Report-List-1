<p><p>The pea protein processing equipment market can be categorized into wet fractionation and dry fractionation methods. Wet fractionation involves using water or aqueous solutions to separate pea proteins from carbohydrates and fibers, resulting in high-quality protein concentrates. In contrast, dry fractionation employs mechanical processes to isolate proteins without the use of solvents or water, yielding a more cost-effective and sustainable option. Both methods cater to the growing demand for plant-based proteins in food, beverage, and nutritional applications.</p></p>
