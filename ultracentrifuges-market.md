<p><p>The ultracentrifuge market is divided into two main types: analytical and preparative. Analytical ultracentrifuges are primarily used for studying the properties of biomolecules, such as their size, shape, and interactions, providing precise data for research. Preparative ultracentrifuges, on the other hand, focus on separating components of mixtures for further analysis or use, often in large-scale applications like protein purification. Both types play crucial roles in biotechnology, pharmaceuticals, and molecular biology research, catering to distinct scientific needs.</p></p>
