<p><p>Lightweight aggregates are materials used in construction to reduce weight without compromising strength. Key types include expansive clay, derived from naturally occurring clay that expands upon heating, creating a lightweight product. Expanded shale is produced by heating shale rock until it expands, resulting in a porous aggregate. Sintered fly ash is made from recycled ash, offering a sustainable option with lightweight properties. Other types may include materials like pumice or expanded glass, each serving specific applications in the construction industry for enhanced performance.</p></p>
