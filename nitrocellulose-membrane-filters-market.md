<p><p>Nitrocellulose membrane filters are categorized based on pore sizes, impacting their application in various fields. Filters with pore sizes below 0.45 µm are typically used for sterilization and clarification of liquids, effectively retaining bacteria and larger particles. Those within the 0.45 to 6 µm range are suitable for applications in microbiological studies and assays, allowing for the filtration of larger particulate matter. Filters with pore sizes above 6 µm are primarily used for pre-filtration and general filtration purposes, enhancing overall filtration efficiency.</p></p>
